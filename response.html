Methodology

Our solution involves creating a platform for children and adolescents to live in a community while still being able to interact with family at work. The design serves as parasitic architecture to an existing office building (In this case the MLC and Governor Phillip Tower) which is in the form of island structures that are modular and interconnecting. This allows the design to be placed on any office building required and modified in size and structure depending on user requirements.
This attachment brings life to the mundane high rises in urban areas and provides an organic balance between a playful environment for the children and the necessities for daily living. The areas will be a space of communal living with registered carers and individuals in other relevant professions to assist around the house for a better quality of life.
The attachments will additionally incorporate automated ‘smart city’ response systems, collecting data about its occupants and feeding this data back to aid the community.

The soft appearance of the parasitic architecture is attractive to children and aesthetically pleasing to look at, while retaining a natural form. Combined with fountains, oases and plentiful flora, it encourages a healthy and rejuvenating lifestyle..

Outcomes

The benefits of the design on the family are enormous as it allows for flexible work/home living conditions for the parents at the early stages of childhood. Some of the outcomes achieved through the design include:

  1. Encouraging faster work recovery time after maternal care due to close proximity with children.
  2. Allowing parents to monitor the development of their children while still in a work environment.
  3. Reducing disparities in parental care, as both parents are able to take care easily.
  4. Opportunities for business to make attachments as a bonus to employment.
  5. Increasing work capacity and work time.
  6. Removing need for travel - eliminating lateness, travel time considerations and the stress of overtime work.
  7. Providing low cost housing in an otherwise expensive area, reducing financial strain on new parents.
  8. The option to rent unaccommodated housing to nearby offices etc.

    9. Precedent Examples

    3XN - Quay Quarter Tower:

    The 200m tall Quay Tower provides an attempt to humanise a highrise. This is through providing an evocative design in which there is a sense of a “vertical village that catalyses life and community feeling”. Instead of one large volume, the design consists of five volumes rotated and stacked with each rotation creating a collection of exterior terraces directly linked to the interior where each volume comprises a village with shared amenity spaces. It is through this that the architecture adds value to highrises both for the users inside and surrounding people.

    MAD Architects - Xinhee Design Center:

    The Xinhee Design center forms a star-shaped layout with six long spanning structures which extend from a central atrium.The building explores the relationship between interior and exterior architecture. It does this by creating mixes office and garden spaces with a layout that creates flexible spaces which allow interaction and communication for each different department.

    Malka Architecture - 3BOX:

    Malka Architecture’s 3BOX democratic housing systems is an example of parasitic architecture in which there exists “prefabricated living spaces which lean on and extend from existing buildings in Paris” which can be extended depending on user needs. This solution is an attempt to introduce low-cost housing to dense cities and fight against urban sprawl.
